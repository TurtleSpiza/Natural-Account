# Spero writing style

The reference the LCC skills point at for prose tone. Materialised here
so the reference resolves within each skill package. This is the
authoritative style for findings narrative, Section-Leader emails,
Finance batch emails, and any workbook commentary.

## 1.0 Sentence construction

Long clause-chaining via commas, confident and direct, never hedges.
A to B to C causation chains, the reasoning is shown not just the
conclusion. Qualifies then commits, state the uncertainty and then
take the position anyway ("the basis is not documented, it is my
belief the 19% is unsupported"). "however" is used mid-sentence, not
as a sentence opener. Casual analogies are acceptable inside otherwise
technical prose where they make a mechanism legible.

## 2.0 Signature phrasing

Natural to this voice: "self-evidently", "arguably", "upon further
investigation", "i.e.,", "coupled with", "thus", "with that said",
"cumulatively speaking", "so to speak", "ought to". Use them where
they fit, do not force them.

## 3.0 Hard rules

Never use em dashes or en dashes anywhere. Use a comma, a full stop,
or rephrase. This is absolute and is machine-enforced by the bundled
linters where they exist.

Australian English throughout (organise, reconcile, recognise,
prioritise, behaviour, programme only where it means a scheme).

Dollar amounts always carry the dollar sign and comma grouping,
$123,456 never 123456. Percentages as figures, 40.3% never "forty
point three percent".

No speculative language in findings, no "should", "could", "may" in a
statement of fact. Recommendations carry the modality, findings do
not. No hedging words, "appears", "possibly", "likely", unless paired
with a specific stated reason. No magnitude adjectives, "significant",
"substantial", "material", without a dollar amount attached to them.

## 4.0 Structure

Numbered sections, 1.0, 2.0, 3.0, with 1.1, 1.2 sub-levels where a
section needs them. Prose, not bullet lists, except where a genuine
register or comparison table is the clearest format. Bold is reserved
for the judgement, the number, or the decision being asked, never for
decoration. Lead with the fact and the dollar amount, then the
evidence, then the recommendation.

## 5.0 Register discipline

Plain, declarative, dollar-amount-led. Active voice. Short sentences
where the point is operational, longer chained sentences where the
reasoning needs to be walked. Do not apologise for length, do not
restate scope, do not narrate the analysis to the reader. The reader
is capable, the job of the prose is to make each point easy to action,
not to perform thoroughness.
