# LCC Finance Batch Email

The standard format for sending outstanding NA analysis items to Finance.

## The one-email rule

All outstanding items go to Amy Forgione and Owen Jensen in a single email. Never drip-feed. Never send one item per email. If a new item is raised after the batch is drafted but before it is sent, fold it in; if after it is sent, hold it for the next batch.

## Recipients

- To: Amy Forgione
- Cc: Owen Jensen

Both are Logan City Council Finance. Either may action; cc'ing Owen keeps the second set of eyes on the batch.

## Subject line

`Parks NA Analysis — outstanding items for Finance action`

Add a count if useful: `Parks NA Analysis — 13 outstanding items for Finance action`.

## Email structure

**Opening (two to three sentences):**

Brief context — the NA analysis is largely complete, a set of items requires Finance action to close, grouped below by type. Do not restate the project scope. Do not apologise for the length.

**Body — one section per action type:**

Group items under headers that name the action needed:

- `Transaction listings requested` — accounts where no listing has been received yet.
- `Source invoices or journal detail requested` — accounts where the listing is in but a specific document is needed.
- `Recodes proposed` — confirmed miscodings where Finance needs to action the journal.
- `Confirmations requested` — clarifications on allocation, contract basis, or period treatment.

**Per-item format:**

Each item is a single paragraph or a single numbered line containing:

1. Account number and name.
2. Period or reference (`FY2025`, `Sep–Nov 2023`, `Period 3`).
3. Specific reference or document identifier (invoice number, journal reference, PO number).
4. Dollar amount, ex-GST, with comma formatting and `$`.
5. The specific ask, in one sentence.

Example:

> **73124 Security** — FY2024 Periods 3 to 6 only received. QLDPSI invoices Sep to Nov 2023 confirmed in 73124 totalling $48,513. Invoices for Dec 2023 to Jun 2024 are not in this account. Please confirm where the remaining nine months of QLDPSI invoices are posted.

> **73130 Waste Disposal** — FY2024 $6,263.10. Journal DR 1-20421-73130 / CR 1-20421-75318 confirmed (your email 2 Aug 2024, svc 20421). Underlying vendor invoice not sighted. Please provide the source invoice.

> **73412 Refund of Operational Contributions** — FY2024 two employee expense reimbursements to Philip Roschitsch. Ref 22052024 $75.21 ACAR licence renewal belongs in 73542. Ref 22072024 $136.96 Ampol fuel belongs in 74183. Please recode both.

**Closing (one or two sentences):**

State when responses are needed by if there is a deadline. Offer to meet or walk through the list if useful. Sign off.

## Drafting checklist

Before sending:

1. Every starred item from the outstanding items register is in the email.
2. Every item names a specific document, reference, or period — no vague asks.
3. Dollar amounts match the workbook exactly.
4. Items are grouped by action type, not by account number.
5. Recodes include both the source account (where it is now) and the target account (where it belongs).
6. The email is one email, not a chain of five.

## Style

Plain, direct. No filler. No apologies for volume. The Finance team is capable; the email's job is to make each item easy to action, not to narrate the analysis.

Dollar signs and comma formatting on every amount. Account numbers and names together on first mention (`73124 Security`, not `73124` alone). Invoice and journal references verbatim — case and punctuation as they appear in the GL.

Short sentences. Active voice. No hedging unless paired with a specific reason.

## What not to include

- The analytical narrative behind each finding — that belongs in the workbook col P, not the email.
- Speculation about why the miscode happened. State the fact, request the action.
- Budget reset recommendations. Those go to Parks management separately.
- Trend commentary. Findings only.

## After sending

Log the send date and the list of items included as a note in the task file session log. Move items from `Outstanding` to `Awaiting Finance response` in the task file. Do not mark anything as closed until Finance confirms.
